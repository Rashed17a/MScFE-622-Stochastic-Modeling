## 📘 Course Overview

This repository contains all Group Work Projects (GWPs) for the **MScFE 622: Stochastic Modeling** course at WorldQuant University. The course covers the mathematical foundations and computational methods for modeling stochastic processes in finance, including option pricing, time series regime detection, and risk-aware sequential portfolio optimization.

## 📂 Projects

### [Project 1 — Black-Scholes Option Pricing & Implied Volatility](./Project_1/)

**Topic:** Black-Scholes model for pricing European options and computing implied volatility from market data.

**Key Topics:**
- Black-Scholes option pricing formula
- Implied volatility calibration from market prices
- Option Greeks: Delta, Gamma, Vega, Theta
- Volatility surface construction
- Numerical root-finding methods (Newton-Raphson, Bisection)

---

### [Project 2 — Time Series Analysis & Regime Detection](./Project_2/)

**Topic:** Analysis of financial time series for market regime shifts using adaptive modeling techniques.

**Key Topics:**
- Financial time series data collection (FRED API)
- S&P 500 and Treasury yield analysis
- Market regime shift detection
- Hidden Markov Models (HMM) for regime identification
- Adaptive finance models for regime-dependent portfolio management

---

### [Project 3 — Risk-Aware Portfolio Selection (Multi-Armed Bandit)](./Project_3/)

**Topic:** Sequential portfolio optimization using a risk-aware Multi-Armed Bandit framework with CVaR.

**Key Topics:**
- Multi-Armed Bandit (MAB) framework for portfolio selection
- UCB1 policy for exploration-exploitation tradeoff
- Conditional Value-at-Risk (CVaR) for risk quantification
- Convex optimization for risk-minimizing portfolios
- Comparison of UCB1, CVaR, and combined portfolio strategies
